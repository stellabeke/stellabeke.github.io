Who I Am

A background in health,
a focus on people

"Most of my work has been about helping people find their footing in complicated situations — and making sure they're not doing it alone."

My background is in public health and programme coordination. I've worked in clinics, government agencies, and business environments — and what's been consistent across all of it is the work with people: figuring out what they need, making resources that actually help, and building spaces where sharing knowledge feels natural.

I care about work that has real purpose behind it. Whether I was coordinating health teams at an airport clinic, running community communications across 17 local governments, or managing a network of 90+ business partners — what kept me going was knowing that the person on the other end was better off because of how the work was done.

How I Work

A few things that matter to me

🌱

Resources That Get Used

I write for the person who's busy and needs it to work the first time. Compliance guides, onboarding docs, practitioner resources — whatever it is, clear and usable matters more than thorough and unread.

🤝

Events That Create Conversation

I've run briefings and community events across large, distributed groups. The goal is always the same: get people actually talking to each other, not just sitting through a presentation.

📊

Measuring What Matters

I'd rather know something isn't working at week two than at the end of a quarter. I build simple tracking systems early and check them regularly — so there's something real to evaluate, not just good intentions.

💬

Writing for the Right Audience

A government compliance update reads very differently than a newsletter for practitioners. Getting the tone right matters — and I've written both, and most things in between.

🏥

Health Is Where I Come From

I've spent most of my career in and around healthcare — clinics, public health offices, alongside practitioners. I know the pressure they're under, and what kind of support actually makes a difference day to day.

⚙️

I Work Well With Ambiguity

A lot of what I've built didn't come with a clear brief. I find that easier to work with, honestly — it's simpler to build something that fits when you're not retrofitting a template made for a different problem.
